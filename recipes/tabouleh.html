Tabbouleh Recipe

Description

Tabouleh is a traditional Middle Eastern salad renowned for its vibrant and fresh flavors. Made with finely chopped parsley, tomatoes, mint, onion, and soaked bulgur, it's dressed with olive oil and lemon juice, creating a refreshing and nutritious dish.

Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 cup finely chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/2 cup soaked bulgur
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped fresh mint
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • Juice of 1-2 lemons
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Rinse and soak bulgur in water for about 30 minutes until it becomes tender. Drain any excess water.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine chopped parsley, soaked bulgur, diced tomatoes, chopped mint, and red onion.
  3. Drizzle extra-virgin olive oil over the mixture and squeeze the juice of 1-2 lemons, adjusting to taste.
  4. Toss the ingredients together until well combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Refrigerate the Tabbouleh for at least 1-2 hours to allow the flavors to meld.
  6. Before serving, give the Tabbouleh a final gentle toss and adjust seasoning if needed.
  7. Serve chilled as a refreshing side dish or a light, healthy salad.
