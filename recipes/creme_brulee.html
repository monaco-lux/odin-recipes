Creme Brulee Recipe

Description

Crème brûlée is a classic French dessert known for its smooth and velvety custard base, infused with vanilla, and topped with a layer of caramelized sugar. The distinctive touch comes from torching the sugar to create a crisp, golden crust that contrasts beautifully with the creamy custard beneath.

Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 vanilla bean, split and seeds scraped (or 1 teaspoon vanilla extract)
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • Extra sugar for caramelizing

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (163°C). Place ramekins in a baking dish and set aside.
  2. In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream and vanilla over medium heat until it simmers. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and sugar until well combined.
  4. Slowly pour the warm cream mixture into the egg mixture, stirring constantly to avoid curdling.
  5. Strain the custard through a fine-mesh sieve into a pouring jug to remove any lumps or the vanilla pod.
  6. Pour the custard into the ramekins in the baking dish and fill the dish with hot water halfway up the sides of the ramekins.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for about 40-45 minutes or until the custard is set but still slightly jiggly in the center.
  8. Remove from the oven and let it cool. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  9. Sprinkle a thin, even layer of sugar on top of each custard and caramelize using a kitchen torch until golden and crispy.
  10. Allow the caramelized sugar to harden, and serve the crème brûlée immediately.
